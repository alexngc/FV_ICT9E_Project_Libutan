What are fires

Fire occurs whenever combustible fuel in the presence of oxygen at an extremely high temperature becomes gas. Flames are the visual indicator of the heated gas. Fire can also occur from lower-temperature sources. Over time, combustible materials such as smoldering embers can reach their ignition temperature.

How do fires start

In the Philippines, faulty wiring is the most common cause of fires. This problem usually comes from old wiring, improper connections, or overloading. Another cause is lit cigarette buts. If you have a family member that smokes, make sure to put out the cigarette properly or just quit. Stove fires are another common cause. Never leave a stove unattended and check the gas range if it's turned off.

How to prevent fires

They say that it is better to be robbed than have your house burned down because in a fire, you lose everything. In order to avoid this, there are several precautionary measures in the table below.

1 Don't smoke Keep flammables from curtains, paper, etc. Do not use open flames for decorations in the house
2 Avoid electrical overloading Check electrical connections for faulty wiring Turn off gas tanks when not in use
3 Install smoke alarms Always have a fire extinguisher ready Keep children off of flammable substances
4 Invest in a quality LPG tank Cords of portable appliances should be as short as possible Remove potential fire hazards
5 Do not leave heated cooking oil unattended Don't cook if you don't know how to Store chemicals properly and avoid mixing them
